Influenza (The Flu)

Influenza is a common viral infection that attacks the respiratory system. It can cause a range of symptoms from mild to severe.

Common Symptoms:

  • Fever or feeling feverish/chills
  • Cough
  • Sore throat
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headaches
  • Fatigue (tiredness)

Prevention Tips:

  • Get an annual flu vaccine.
  • Wash your hands frequently with soap and water.
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth.
  • Clean and disinfect surfaces and objects.

Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs either when the pancreas does not produce enough insulin or when the body cannot effectively use the insulin it produces.

Common Symptoms:

  • Frequent urination
  • Increased thirst
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Increased hunger
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing sores

Prevention Tips:

  • Maintain a healthy body weight.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables.
  • Engage in regular physical activity.
  • Limit your intake of sugar and saturated fats.

Dengue Fever

Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne tropical disease caused by the dengue virus. Symptoms typically begin three to fourteen days after infection.

Common Symptoms:

  • High fever
  • Severe headache
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Muscle and joint pains
  • Rash
  • Mild bleeding from the gums or nose

Prevention Tips:

  • Wear long-sleeved shirts and pants.
  • Use mosquito repellent.
  • Use mosquito nets when sleeping.
  • Eliminate stagnant water sources where mosquitoes can breed.
